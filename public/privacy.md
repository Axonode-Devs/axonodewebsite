# Axonode: Privacy Policy

*Last Updated:* [30.06.2026]

## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Axonode ("we," "us," "the Organization") collects, uses, stores, and protects information from applicants, members, and visitors to our website. By applying to Axonode, joining as a Member, or using our website, you agree to the practices described here.

## 2. Who Can Use Axonode

Axonode is open to individuals aged 15 and older, primarily high school and university students. We do not knowingly collect information from individuals under 15. If we become aware that someone under 15 has submitted an application, we will delete the associated data.

Applicants Under 18
If an applicant indicates they are under 18, We will attempt to notify the parent or guardian about the application and, where needed, communicate with them on the applicant's behalf. If we are unable to establish this contact including cases where the applicant is unwilling or unable to provide it we may decline or discontinue the application. Data collected from an applicant later found to be under 15 will be deleted in accordance with the paragraph above.

## 3. Information We Collect

### 3.1 Information You Provide at Application

When you apply to Axonode through the application form on our website, we collect:

- Full name
- Email address
- Birthdate(used to determine applicable age-related procedures)
- Parent/guardian contact information, if applicant is under 18
- Phone number and/or other communication handles (e.g. Instagram)
- Area of interest (Technology, Design, Business & Strategy, Creative Arts)
- Self assessed skill level (Junior, Mid, Senior)
- English proficiency
- Motivation letter, where relevant to the application

### 3.2 Blog Comments

Axonode operates a self-hosted WordPress blog connected to Members' existing Axonode accounts — there is no separate blog-specific account or login. If you comment on a blog post, we collect the content of your comment, the timestamp, and your IP address, which we retain for 90 days for spam and abuse prevention. Blog posts and comments are public by default; Members should not include confidential or project-sensitive information in blog posts or comments, as these are intended for public-facing community updates.

### 3.3 Community Chat (WhatsApp)
 
Our primary community chat currently operates through a WhatsApp group, which members join via invite link. Where possible, we configure group privacy settings to limit visibility of phone numbers between members. This platform is subject to change — we may move to a different chat platform or a tool built by Axonode in the future — and this Policy will be updated to reflect the platform in active use. Regardless of platform, community chat is not treated as a system of record for project data — see Section 6.

### 3.4 Remote Events
 
We use video calling tools (such as Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, or Zoom, depending on what best suits the members involved) for community-building calls. These calls are live by default. A call may be recorded only with the active, informed consent of participants obtained before recording begins; anyone who does not consent may still participate without the call being recorded, or may decline to participate. Recordings are retained for 30 days and accessible only to the Axonode Board. Separately, if a participant consents, short clips from a recording may be shared publicly (e.g. for promotional purposes); this is a distinct consent from the recording consent itself and will be requested separately. The specific tool used for a given call is a data processor for that call only; we may vary the tool used based on member familiarity or preference.

### 3.5 Information Collected Automatically
 
When you visit our homepage, we use *Google Search Console* to understand how visitors find our site through search (e.g. search queries, click-through data, indexing status). We do not currently use tools that track individual visitor behavior across our site.

### 3.6 Project Related Information

Members who develop projects within Axonode generate project data as described in our Terms of Service and Code of Conduct, including project content, history, and internal discussion related to the project. We may, in the future, collect *usage analytics* (such as page views and time spent) on websites or products built through Axonode projects. This Policy will be updated before any such analytics are implemented.

## 4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information collected to:

- Evaluate applications and determine fit for Axonode
- Communicate with applicants and members
- Organize members into projects based on skill, interest, and experience level
- Operate, maintain, and improve the Axonode website
- Understand how visitors find our site through search performance data
- Enforce our Terms of Service and Code of Conduct, including reviewing relevant communications or project data during a dispute or conduct investigation, as authorized by the Board

## 5. Third Party Services
 
We work with the following third-party service providers, who process certain data on our behalf:
 
- *Resend:* used to send application confirmations, communications, and other organizational emails
- *Google Search Console:* used to understand how visitors find our homepage through search
- *WhatsApp (Meta)*: our community chat currently operates through WhatsApp. By joining, your phone number, messages, and profile information are processed by WhatsApp/Meta according to their own privacy policy, which we do not control. We encourage members to review WhatsApp's privacy policy directly. If we change chat platforms in the future, this entry will be updated accordingly.
- *Video calling tools (e.g. Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Zoom):* used for Remote Events, subject to the recording practices described in Section 3.4. The specific tool may vary; each operates according to its own provider's privacy policy for the portion of data it processes

*We may use additional third-party tools from time to time to operate Axonode (e.g. scheduling or communication tools). Where a new tool is introduced that meaningfully changes how your data is processed, we will update this list and notify members where appropriate.*
 
These providers process data according to their own privacy policies and applicable data protection standards. *We do not sell member or applicant data to third parties.*
 


## 6. Data Retention

- Application data is retained for as long as necessary to evaluate your application and, if accepted, for the duration of your membership.
- Project data is retained in accordance with our Terms of Service, including the "No Takedown" rule. Because Axonode preserves the history and integrity of the community's work, the project itself and its development history remain archived even after a member leaves the Organization. However, a departing member may request removal of their personal data — including their name, contact information, and attribution — from project history and discussions, and we will honor such requests. Where this occurs, affected contributions will be credited generically rather than to the individual.
- Blog comments are treated the same as other personal data under this Policy — see Section 8 for removal requests.
- Project-relevant discussion and decisions may be archived on Axonode-managed platforms (e.g. our project portal) for continuity purposes. WhatsApp itself is not treated as a system of record, and we do not guarantee retention or retrievability of WhatsApp messages.
- Analytics data collected via Google Search Console is retained according to Google's standard retention settings.


## 7. Confidentiality of Internal Discussions

Axonode communications and content fall into three categories:

- Confidential (default): Internal project discussions and materials not marked "Public" by the Board. We do not disclose this content outside the Organization except where required to enforce our Code of Conduct, resolve disputes, or comply with legal obligations.
- Member-visible: Content shared within community channels such as our WhatsApp group, visible to other members but not intended for distribution outside Axonode.
- Public: Content explicitly marked "Public" by the Board, or published to public-facing channels such as blog posts and blog comments, which are visible to anyone.

## 8. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal information. The "No Takedown" rule protects the archived project work and its development history, which remains part of Axonode's records regardless of membership status. It does not, however, prevent you from requesting the removal of your personal data — including your name, contact details, and attribution — from that history. Such requests can be directed to the Board and will be honored, with affected contributions credited generically going forward.

## 9. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect the personal information we collect from unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as Axonode grows and our practices evolve, including as new features like project usage analytics are introduced. Material changes will be communicated to members where appropriate. Continued use of Axonode after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.
